Why Epoxy

More Than
a Coating.

Epoxy flooring transforms a bare concrete slab into a surface that's durable, chemical-resistant, easy to clean, and genuinely beautiful. Done right, an epoxy floor will outlast the concrete beneath it.

The key word is done right. Most epoxy failures come from one cause: inadequate surface preparation. We grind and profile every slab to the correct surface profile before we ever open a bucket of coating.

Our Process

How It
Works.

01
Free On-Site Estimate

We come to your location, inspect the slab, test for moisture, assess any cracks, and give you a detailed, accurate quote with no hidden fees.

02
Surface Grinding & Prep

The most important step. We grind the concrete to create a mechanical bond profile. Cracks and imperfections are repaired. Expansion joints are addressed.

03
Primer / Base Coat

Professional-grade epoxy primer penetrates the concrete and creates the foundation layer. Applied at correct temperature and humidity for maximum adhesion.

04
Decorative Layer

Flakes are broadcast, metallic pigments are applied, or solid color topcoat is laid — depending on your chosen system.

05
Topcoat & Cure

A durable polyaspartic or polyurea topcoat is applied. We walk you through care instructions. Light foot traffic in 24 hrs; vehicles in 72 hrs.

Why Diamond Grinding Matters

Acid etching only opens the concrete's pores. Diamond grinding creates a true mechanical bond — the same surface profile used in industrial and commercial applications. This is why our floors don't peel.

The #1 cause is inadequate surface prep. If the contractor acid-etches only instead of grinding, the coating has nothing to mechanically grip. Moisture vapor from an untested slab is the second leading cause. We address both before applying any coating.
A standard 2–3 car garage typically takes 1–2 days. Larger or more complex jobs are scoped individually. We'll give you a clear timeline during your free estimate.
Light foot traffic is generally safe within 24 hours. We recommend waiting 72 hours before driving on it. Full chemical cure takes 7 days — avoid spills of harsh chemicals before then.
Standard epoxy can be susceptible to hot tire pickup. That's why we apply a polyaspartic or polyurea topcoat on all garage floor systems — these topcoats are 4x harder than epoxy and fully resistant to hot tire transfer.
